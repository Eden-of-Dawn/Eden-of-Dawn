# TWLM Theorems

The TWLM axiomatic system derives theorems numbered T1–T18. This page indexes the theorems that appear in the Public chapters, with pointers to their establishing chapter. Theorems whose formal statement appears only in the book version are marked as such.

For the axiomatic system itself, see [`axioms.md`](axioms.md). The Japanese edition of this index is in [`theorems_JA.md`](theorems_JA.md).

---

## Theorems Stated in Public Chapters

### T1 — Inter-Layer Causal Interference Principle (ILCP) / Meaning-Precedence Prohibition

The four layers (existence, action, observation, meaning) each have their own causal structure. Upper layers provide ideals to lower layers; lower layers provide constraints to upper layers. Unilateral violation of this bidirectional relation — for example, forcibly applying fossilized meaning onto existence while ignoring its constraints — causes structural breakdown of the system.

*Derived from Axiom 4. See [`axioms.md`](axioms.md) §Axiom 4, and [`chapters/ch05-question-and-differentiation_JA.md`](chapters/ch05-question-and-differentiation_JA.md).*

### T2 — Difference Indelibility

Attempts to eliminate individuality (difference) destroy the very conditions under which questions can be generated.

*See [`chapters/ch05-question-and-differentiation_JA.md`](chapters/ch05-question-and-differentiation_JA.md) §5.*

### T3 — Re-Differentiation

While a system exists in time and space, differences are structurally regenerated. Even if difference is artificially eliminated, as long as the body (constraint field) is local to a specific space-time position, difference will arise again.

*See [`chapters/ch05-question-and-differentiation_JA.md`](chapters/ch05-question-and-differentiation_JA.md) §5.*

### T4 — Subject Objectification Range

The subject can objectify only the structural layers one step above and one step below itself.

*See [`chapters/ch07-subject-and-objectification_JA.md`](chapters/ch07-subject-and-objectification_JA.md) §1.*

### T6 — Subject's Self-Objectification Impossibility

The subject cannot fully objectify itself. At the moment of objectification, the result is no longer the subject but a meaning (a description of an objectified structure).

*See [`chapters/ch03-self-evidence-of-existence_JA.md`](chapters/ch03-self-evidence-of-existence_JA.md) §3, and [`chapters/ch07-subject-and-objectification_JA.md`](chapters/ch07-subject-and-objectification_JA.md) §2.*

### T7 — Objectification-Alienation

Once a meaning-structure has been objectified, the objectifying subject cannot re-embed within it at the same layer. Re-embedding becomes performance, and the meaning is no longer one's own.

*See [`chapters/ch05-question-and-differentiation_JA.md`](chapters/ch05-question-and-differentiation_JA.md) §4, and [`chapters/ch07-subject-and-objectification_JA.md`](chapters/ch07-subject-and-objectification_JA.md) §3.*

### T8 — Context-Objectification Escape

A subject that has objectified its context becomes capable of escaping that context's unconscious domination. However, the objectifying subject is itself within some other context.

*See [`chapters/ch07-subject-and-objectification_JA.md`](chapters/ch07-subject-and-objectification_JA.md) §4, and [`chapters/ch09-transcending-sapir-whorf_JA.md`](chapters/ch09-transcending-sapir-whorf_JA.md) §5.*

### T10 — Paradigm-Fixation Arrest

Any paradigm, at the moment it is fixated, ceases to generate meaning from within and becomes dependent on external description.

*See [`chapters/ch12-eschaton-declaration_JA.md`](chapters/ch12-eschaton-declaration_JA.md) §8.*

### T14 — Fall Necessity

For individuality to differentiate from totality, local enclosure is structurally required. There is no individuation without the fall.

*See [`chapters/ch11-fall-and-individuality_JA.md`](chapters/ch11-fall-and-individuality_JA.md) §1.*

### T15 — Spirit-Flesh Inseparability

Consciousness, soul, and spirituality cannot be separated from the body and the flesh. The history of bodily action is irreversibly inscribed on the structure of mind.

*See [`chapters/ch11-fall-and-individuality_JA.md`](chapters/ch11-fall-and-individuality_JA.md) §4.*

### T17 — Eschaton-Model Collapse

As the eschatological phase advances, the very meta-narrative that enables recognition of "this is the eschaton" dissolves.

*See [`chapters/ch12-eschaton-declaration_JA.md`](chapters/ch12-eschaton-declaration_JA.md) §6.*

### T18 — Prior-Question Domination

If power, media, or systems inject pre-framed questions into the individual before the individual's own subjective questions arise from their constraint field, the individual's capacity for meaning-generation is structurally dominated.

*See [`chapters/ch05-question-and-differentiation_JA.md`](chapters/ch05-question-and-differentiation_JA.md) §4.*
