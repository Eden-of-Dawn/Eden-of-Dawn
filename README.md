# TWLM — Transcendent World Life Model

> TWLM is a research program deriving the structural conditions of meaning generation from a unique-subject observer foundation. Five axioms, eighteen theorems, and a three-phase historical schema of meaning emergence and collapse.

**Status:** v1.0 manuscript complete. Public materials are being released in stages.

**Languages:** **English** | [日本語](README_JA.md)

---

## What TWLM Delivers

- A formal axiomatic system: **five axioms** on questioning, difference, and meaning.
- **Eighteen derived theorems** covering meaning emergence, saturation, and collapse.
- A **three-phase historical schema** of meaning, each phase terminated by an *eschaton* where the prior meaning-generating structure becomes untenable.
- The **Inter-Layer Causal Interference Principle (ILCP)**, formalizing how semantic and existential layers mutually constrain one another.
- A diagnosis of the present phase: external sense-givers have become structurally unavailable, requiring a turn to self-generated meaning under the unique-subject premise.

## The Three Eschatons

TWLM identifies three historical phases of meaning, each terminated by an *eschaton* — a structural saturation point after which the prior regime of meaning can no longer sustain itself.

- **Phase I — Body → Reason.** Meaning is anchored in bodily-ancestral law. The first eschaton is the Mosaic–Christic transition, where the externality of ritual law becomes structurally untenable and is superseded by a rational-universal regime.
- **Phase II — Reason → Spirit.** Meaning is anchored in rational-universal structures. The second eschaton is the modern / late-modern transition, where universal reason becomes self-undermining and is superseded by the demand for spiritual-individual meaning.
- **Phase III — Spirit.** The present. External sense-givers are no longer available. Meaning must be generated from within, under the unique-subject premise. This is the phase in which TWLM's §6 operates.
