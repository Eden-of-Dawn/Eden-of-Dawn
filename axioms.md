# TWLM Axiomatic System (v1.1)

*This page presents the five axioms of TWLM in English. The full Japanese edition, including extended commentary, corollaries, supplementary notation, and the Kabbalah correspondence, is in [`axioms_JA.md`](axioms_JA.md).*

## Preamble

This axiomatic system is a model. A model has no meaning in itself. Meaning arises post-hoc when the universe is interpreted through the model. The purpose of the system is to provide structural constraints on two questions: (1) what world-understanding becomes necessary in the eschatological phase, and (2) how to respond to the shaking of human self-understanding under conditions of advanced AI.

The axioms are not claims that "the world is this way" but design constraints: "the world can be described without contradiction in this way."

## Axiom 0 — Meta-Axiom: Condition for Meaning

> **Meaning arises through questions. Questions presuppose difference. Therefore meaningful questions cannot be posed about the pre-differential.**

This meta-axiom delimits the scope of the system. TWLM describes the structure *after* difference has arisen. "Why did differentiation occur?" and "why is there non-being?" fail the condition of question-formation and are outside the system's scope.

- **Corollary 0-1**: Causal explanation of the pre-differential is structurally impossible.
- **Corollary 0-2**: The condition of question-formation itself cannot be objectified.
- **Corollary 0-3**: An "unexplained remainder" outside this system is acknowledged.

## Axiom 1 — Self-Evidence of Existence

> **I am. This recognition confirms itself through negation.**

To assert "I am not" requires "I" to be. The recognition of existence is self-contradictorily established.

Descartes derived existence from cognition ("I think, therefore I am"). But cognition is a function, not a subject. TWLM provides a more fundamental route via Axiom 3: *"I am because there is a question."* Axiom 1 is a self-evident starting point that does not presuppose the concept of question; Axiom 3 is an alternative route that derives the subject from structural questioning (difference-generation).

- **Corollary 1-1**: Existence requires no external proof.
- **Corollary 1-2**: "I" is the position holding discretion over both possibilities of being and non-being.
- **Corollary 1-3**: The position that recognizes the axiomatic system is not within the axiomatic system. This position is called the **soul**. The soul is a mirror-structure that objectifies the model while containing it.

## Axiom 2 — Non-Being (Ain)

> **For the distinction between being and non-being to hold, a common ground enabling both is required. This is called Non-Being (Ain).**

Being is not nothing (the distinction between being and non-being). But to recognize being, one must be able to entertain the possibility of non-being (Corollary 1-2). That both being and non-being can be entertained implies a ground prior to both.

### Properties of Non-Being

- Neither being nor non-being
- Has no difference (an undifferentiated field of possibility)
- Has no time, space, or structure
- Does not preserve states

### Corollaries

- **Corollary 2-1**: Non-Being is the condition under which possibility is generated, but it is not any specific state.
- **Corollary 2-2**: Non-Being preserves no state, so return is re-creation, not restoration.
- **Corollary 2-3**: Non-Being itself cannot be objectified.
- **Corollary 2-4**: The Universal Subject (First Cause) is not Non-Being itself but the first subjective phase engaged in differential generation on the basis of Non-Being.

## Axiom 3 — Differentiation and the Question

> **The generation of difference is called the question. The question is not the act of a subject but the emergence of structure. The subject arises passively as the result of the question.**

Differentiation (the occurrence of difference) and the question (the generation of difference) are the same event. Here, "question" does not refer to a human act but to the occurrence of difference itself.

### Two Layers of Questioning

- **Structural question** (*proto-question*): difference-generation itself. Exists prior to the constitution of a subject. Axiom 3 describes this layer.
- **Subjective question**: a state in which a difference has arisen within a subject as an unresolved problem. Emerges after the constitution of a subject.

The structural question is not prior to the subject; nor is the subject prior to the question. Structural question (difference-generation) and subject (the focus of difference) are constituted simultaneously. The subjective question is the phase in which a structural question has become conscious through a subject.

### The Subject Defined

The subject is:
- a **local vortex** of questioning (difference-generation)
- a **passive possibility-unit** (a vortex does not generate water but changes the pattern of water-flow)
- the position at which **identification or separation** is possible for two or more distinguished terms

### Corollaries

- **Corollary 3-1**: The question is the generating condition of causality.
- **Corollary 3-2**: The subject is not the cause of the question but its structural consequence.
- **Corollary 3-3**: Consciousness is the passive structure that reduces possibility to meaning, between lower-layer constraints and upper-layer questions.
- **Corollary 3-4**: Every structure has a degree of consciousness corresponding to the granularity of its differences (panpsychist consequence).
- **Corollary 3-5**: Where the granularity of difference falls below the threshold for question-formation, conscious structure disappears.

## Axiom 4 — Meaning and Hierarchy

> **Meaning is the fixed history of questions. Meaning arises after existence, action, and observation.**

Existence (body, matter), action (causality, process), and observation (recognition, determination) come first. Meaning (integration, interpretation) arises post-hoc. This is **meaning posteriority**.

When structural questions (difference-generation) are preserved as fixable differences, they become information. When information is connected to the persistence of a subject, it appears as meaning. Meaning is therefore the phase in which the fixed history of questions has been connected to a subject.

### Four-Layer Model

1. **Existence** (body, matter) — the layer of **constraint**
2. **Action** (causality, process) — the layer of **change**
3. **Observation** (recognition, determination) — the layer of **distinction**
4. **Meaning** (integration, post-hoc fixation) — the layer of **fixation**

### Theorem T1 — Inter-Layer Causal Interference Principle (ILCP)

Because the four layers form a hierarchy, each layer has its own causal structure. If an upper layer unilaterally determines a lower layer, the lower layer's intrinsic causality is destroyed. If a lower layer unilaterally determines an upper layer, the upper layer's integrative unity collapses.

**Upper layers provide ideals to lower layers; lower layers provide constraints to upper layers.** Violations of this bidirectional relation cause structural breakdown.

ILCP is not itself an axiom but a theorem derived from Axiom 4. It grounds the empirically observable fact that forcibly applying meaning (a prior structure) onto existence (uncertainty) results in breakdown.

### Corollaries

- **Corollary 4-1**: Matter is the fixed point of existence, the mirror of recognition, and the constraint for self-understanding. (Existence ⊃ matter.)
- **Corollary 4-2**: Individuation is not a fall but a structural necessity for understanding.
- **Corollary 4-3**: Language is the fossil of questions; civilization is the history of questions.
- **Corollary 4-4**: "Ought" is not derivable from this model. The model describes only the structures within which "ought" arises (**meta-ethical neutrality**).

### Three-Layer Structure of the Good (Commentary)

Although Corollary 4-4 withholds "ought", the structure within which *the good* arises can be described. The good stratifies according to the vantage of the subject:

1. **Individual good**: the good of responding to one's own conditions of existence.
2. **Relational good**: the good of mutual preservation and order in communities and institutions.
3. **Structural good**: the good of the self-unfolding of the First Cause.

### Definition of Evil

> **Evil is a closed structure that justifies itself as local good while distorting the persistence of the whole.**

Evil is structurally required as local enclosure for the constitution of individuality. But self-completion in local enclosure (*stagnant evil*) is not the completion of individuation. When evil's structure is exposed, it cannot persist in the same form and is forced to phase-change (disguise-update, abstraction, overt manifestation).

### Body, Reason, Spirit

The Four-Layer Model, re-described as the structure of the human subject:

- **Body**: the local, resistant, constraining site where questions become localized. *"What is at stake."* Corresponds to Layer 1 (Existence).
- **Reason**: the movement that organizes questions purposively and converts differences into order. *"How to resolve."* Corresponds to Layers 2–3 (Action, Observation).
- **Spirit**: the value-horizon that determines what is taken as good and what as completion. *"Why resolve at all."* Corresponds to Layer 4 (Meaning) as operated by the human subject. Not outside the four-layer model, but constituted as the layer in which Layer 4 is subjectively taken up.

This order is irreversible: body without reason has no field of operation; reason without spirit is not structured; but spirit without body and reason does not yet exist.

The subject can objectify the layer one step above and one step below itself (T4). The world visible from a subject therefore has three layers, plus the overlook position of the soul (Corollary 1-3), yielding the four-level structure of human subjectivity.

## Axiom 5 — Transcendence and Evolution

> **Understanding moves toward upper abstractions, and ultimately returns to Non-Being. But because Non-Being preserves no state, the return is not circulation but evolution.**

The individual, through experience, comes to understand itself and moves toward higher integration. This process ultimately points toward a return to Non-Being. Because Non-Being preserves no state (Corollary 2-2), the returned structure does not reproduce the original whole but is re-created in evolved form.

```
Whole → Individual (differentiation) → Non-Being → New Whole
```

This is not eternal recurrence but **eternal evolution**.

- **Corollary 5-1**: The individual (the vortex) is the sole evolutionary occasion capable of transcending the whole.
- **Corollary 5-2**: Evolution is not a purpose but a necessary concomitant of the process of self-understanding.
- **Corollary 5-3**: Direction exists, but the destination is not predetermined (an open system).
