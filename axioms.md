# The Five Axioms

*This page is a placeholder. The definitive statement of the five axioms will be published here as part of the staged release.*

The five axioms of TWLM concern **questioning**, **difference**, **the unique subject**, **meaning generation**, and **historical phase**. They are derived and stated formally in the book manuscript and summarized in the six-pager.

## Scope

The axiomatic system is designed to be minimal but load-bearing: each axiom is required for at least one non-trivial theorem in the eighteen-theorem stack, and no axiom is derivable from the others. The unique-subject axiom is the structurally upstream choice that distinguishes TWLM from distributed-observer programs.
